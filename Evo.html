The Evolution of Samsung Galaxy Watches

Samsung’s journey in the smartwatch industry is one of bold experimentation, technological leadership, and continuous refinement. From the earliest prototypes to the modern Galaxy Watch series, Samsung has built one of the most influential smartwatch lineups in tech.

2013 — Samsung Galaxy Gear: The Beginning

Samsung’s smartwatch journey officially began in 2013 with the Samsung Galaxy Gear.

Key Features:

  • 1.63-inch Super AMOLED display
  • 1.9 MP camera built into the strap
  • Voice control with S Voice
  • Exclusive to Samsung Galaxy phones

2014 — Gear 2, Gear 2 Neo and Gear Fit: Experiments Begin

Samsung expanded rapidly by launching multiple models, testing designs and use cases.

Samsung’s Strategy:

  • Study fitness and notifications usage
  • Test different shapes and styles
  • Collect real user data for improvement

2015 — Gear S2: The Rotating Bezel

Samsung introduced the rotating bezel, a fast and intuitive way to navigate apps.

  • First circular Samsung watch
  • Rotating bezel UI
  • Tizen OS optimized for watches
  • Improved battery life

2016–2017 — Gear S3 and Gear Sport: Refinement

More durability, bigger batteries, and better sensors made this era feel more premium.

  • Rugged build and premium design
  • Standalone GPS and LTE models
  • Better always on display experience

2018 — Galaxy Watch: The Name Change

Samsung renamed the lineup to Galaxy Watch to unify branding and strengthen the ecosystem.

  • Stronger Galaxy identity
  • Better ecosystem marketing
  • Improved design and battery focus

2020 — Galaxy Watch 3: Health Breakthrough

Health features became the focus during this era of smart wellness tracking.

  • SpO2 monitoring
  • ECG readings
  • Fall detection
  • Advanced sleep scoring

2021 — Galaxy Watch 4 Series: Wear OS Returns

Samsung partnered with Google, shifting from Tizen to Wear OS for more apps and better Android integration.

  • Google Play app access
  • Stronger Android integration
  • Samsung hardware plus Google ecosystem

2022–2023 — Galaxy Watch 5 Pro and Watch 6: The Modern Era

Premium materials, better durability, smarter tracking, and improved performance defined this era.

  • Titanium body and sapphire glass
  • Enhanced GPS and battery life
  • Improved health insights and sensors
  • Stronger ecosystem connection
